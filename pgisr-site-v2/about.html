The Origin

How PGI Was Conceived

Precision Ground Intelligence was born from a simple observation: the security industry was full of sensor manufacturers, and almost none of them thought beyond the hardware. Operators were drowning in data — alerts, signals, feeds — but starving for answers.

The founders of PGI, drawing on decades of defense electronics experience, set out to build something different — not a sensor company, but an intelligence company. A system that begins at the perimeter and ends with a command-ready decision, with every step engineered, integrated, and supported by the same team.

The result is PGISR — a persistent ground intelligence platform purpose-built for the environments and missions where failure is not an option. Scalable by design, ruggedized for impact and immersion, rated for wide operating temperatures, and deployable anywhere without external power or infrastructure.

The Manufacturing Foundation

Backed by Six Decades of
Precision Electronics Manufacturing

PGI is the intelligence platform division of Precision Wichita, Inc. (PWI) — a US electronics manufacturer founded in 1963 in Wichita, Kansas. PWI has spent over six decades designing and producing precision electronics for aerospace, defense, and industrial applications.

That manufacturing heritage is built into every PGISR unit. The same quality systems, certifications, and engineering discipline that support aerospace programs — ISO 9001, AS9100D, ITAR, FAA repair station certification — govern every sensor we ship.

No contract manufacturing. No offshore components. No shortcuts. Every unit is designed, assembled, and tested in Wichita — because that's how PWI has operated since 1963, and it's how PGI operates today.
